Bio

I want to build scalable brain stimulation technology that precisely engages with mechanisms of human cognition and restores function in psychiatric disorders. My research goal is to overcome the current limitations of non-invasive interaction with the brain to enable more effective neurotechnological treatments. To reach that goal, I want to engineer stimulation methods with high spatial and temporal specificity that can adapt to ongoing brain states and activity. Moreover, I want to develop novel computational tools to identify potential stimulation targets and maximize intervention effects. My active involvement in various research groups specialized in neuroscience, medical engineering and psychology demonstrates my comprehensive training in research and my deep interest in developing more precise methods and personalized interventions.
